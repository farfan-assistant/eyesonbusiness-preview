FAQ

The questions local businesses usually ask before they commit.

A good landing page should lower friction, not create more of it. These are the questions that matter before a business owner says yes to a postcard spot.

What exactly are you selling?
A shared postcard campaign for local businesses. Multiple non-competing advertisers share one mailer, which keeps costs down while still giving each business a real physical presence in targeted neighborhoods.
Which businesses are the best fit?
Local service businesses, restaurants, salons, wellness brands, dentists, realtors, and other businesses with a clear local radius and a simple homeowner offer tend to fit best.
How do you choose the neighborhoods?
Routes are selected first. We look at the household profile, route quality, and the type of business being featured so the card makes sense for the people receiving it.
Is this replacing Facebook or Google ads?
No. The better positioning is additive. Direct mail gives a business local physical visibility in the mailbox, while digital can still do its own job. They work well together.
Do I share the card with competitors?
No. The working model is one business per category per card. That keeps the postcard cleaner and protects the value of the spot.
When do you collect payment?
Before the spot is reserved and before design starts. That keeps the campaign real, protects production workflow, and avoids speculative holds that slow everything down.
Can response be tracked?
Yes. The strongest version includes a dynamic QR code and a clear offer so scan activity and response can be reviewed after the drop.
What do you need from me to get started?
Usually your logo, your main offer, contact details, and a quick sense of your ideal customer. If the offer is weak, we would rather tighten it before the card goes to print.
